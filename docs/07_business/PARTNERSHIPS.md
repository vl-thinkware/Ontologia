# Partnerships

How Ontologia grows with and through other companies — technology alliances, channel partners, systems integrators, communities, and academic ties.

---

## 1. Why partners matter

- Our TAM includes many organisations we cannot reach alone.
- Buyers of ontology tools increasingly buy *ecosystems*, not point tools.
- Partners amplify credibility with enterprises where a 30-person company cannot stand alone.
- Integrations widen the moat: Ontologia becomes the hub in a larger workflow.

## 2. Partner categories

| Category | Examples | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Technology alliances | Neo4j, Stardog, Ontotext (GraphDB), AWS Neptune | Integrations, co-marketing, credibility |
| Cloud marketplaces | AWS, Azure, GCP | Procurement ease, enterprise reach |
| Systems integrators (SIs) | Thoughtworks, EPAM, Slalom, Capgemini, boutique data firms | Implementation, referrals, scale |
| Data catalogue partners | Collibra, Atlan, Alation, DataHub | Complementary integrations |
| Ontology / vocabulary communities | Schema.org, W3C, CIDOC-CRM, SNOMED International | Standards alignment, community trust |
| Academic / research | Universities, research labs | Citations, talent pipeline |
| Referral / affiliate | Independent consultants, niche agencies | Low-touch acquisition |

## 3. Partnership principles

- **Mutual value**. We only build a partnership if both sides measurably benefit.
- **Customer first**. Partnerships never override the customer's interest. No lock-in of customers into a partner's stack against their will.
- **Open by default**. Documented APIs and exports mean integrations are replicable; we don't hide interfaces.
- **Clear accountability**. Every partnership has named owners on both sides, joint goals, and a cadence.
- **Honesty**. We don't exaggerate partner depth. "Integration available" only when shippable.

## 4. Technology alliances — Neo4j (priority)

Neo4j is our default graph DB. We commit to:

- First-class export to Cypher, with deployment helpers for Neo4j Aura.
- Joint webinars and conference presence (Knowledge Graph Conference, NODES).
- Reference integration in Neo4j Labs samples.
- Potential long-term listing on Neo4j's partner program.

Neo4j commits to:

- Technical contacts for joint deployments.
- Co-marketing slots.
- Solution partner listing on their website.

## 5. Technology alliances — other graph vendors

- **Stardog**: publish a reference integration; participate in their community events. Useful for RDF-heavy customers.
- **GraphDB (Ontotext)**: publish import/export path for RDF/OWL artefacts.
- **AWS Neptune, Azure Cosmos DB**: maintain export paths and document deployments.
- **TigerGraph**: document export and case study.

Every integration listed on our site carries a maturity tag: **Reference**, **Production-ready**, **Community-maintained**.

## 7. Systems integrators

### Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| Registered | Training + 1 signed MSA | Partner portal, discounted seats for consultants, deal registration |
| Silver | $200k / yr influenced | + Co-marketing, SDR shared account list |
| Gold | $1M / yr influenced | + Executive sponsor, dedicated partner manager, early product access |

### Onboarding
- Partner training programme (2 days).
- Certification exam.
- Access to demo environments.
- Co-branded deck and case studies.

### Deal registration
- Submitted via the partner portal.
- Approved within 48 h.
- Protection window: 90 days.
- Gross margin share: 20% year 1, 10% year 2, 0% thereafter.

### Joint success practices
- Quarterly business reviews.
- Jointly owned accounts with clear RACI.
- Shared Slack or Teams channel per active account.

## 8. Data catalogue and governance partners

We coexist with data catalogues. Integration patterns:

- Ontologia concepts visible in the catalogue with a backlink.
- Catalogue assets taggable with Ontologia terms.
- Events from Ontologia (commit, merge) can feed catalogue workflows.

Partnerships start as integrations. If both sides see pipeline benefit, we formalise co-marketing.

## 9. Standards bodies & communities

- **W3C**: follow and contribute to RDF, SKOS, SHACL updates.
- **Schema.org**: support import and mapping.
- **CIDOC-CRM**: cultural heritage ontology; support mapping.
- **SNOMED International**: where customers require it.
- **Open Ontology Repository, NCBO BioPortal**: cross-linking and imports.

No pay-to-play; participation is via technical contribution and presence at community forums.

## 10. Academic and research partnerships

- Free Pro licenses for accredited academic research.
- Co-authored papers and case studies.
- Sponsored research projects in targeted fields (pharma ontologies, public-sector metadata).
- Internship and fellowship programme with 2–3 universities per year.

## 11. Referral / affiliate

- Open referral programme: signup and get a trackable link.
- Payout: 20% of first-year revenue for qualified closed deals, or $2k flat for smaller self-serve conversions.
- Payable via Stripe Connect; reporting in-app.
- Fraud controls and clawbacks documented in the programme terms.

## 12. Partner operations

- **Partner manager** role from month 9, initially covered by the CEO.
- **Partner portal** (post-seed): deal registration, training, collateral.
- **MDF (market development funds)** pool for qualified partners running demand-gen campaigns with us.
- **Joint marketing kit**: decks, one-pagers, email templates, social assets.

## 13. Legal & commercial baseline

- Partner agreements based on a standard MSA with channel addendum.
- No exclusivity clauses in either direction.
- Confidentiality safeguards around deal registration.
- Data sharing limited to what is needed for deal execution.

## 14. Metrics

- Partner-sourced ARR.
- Partner-influenced ARR.
- Number of active partners (deal registrations in the last 90 days).
- Joint pipeline velocity compared to direct.
- Partner satisfaction (survey, twice a year).

## 15. Partner risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Deal conflict | Deal registration + clear RACI + escalation path |
| Over-reliance on one partner | Diversification across categories and geographies |
| Partner drives customer into a lock-in stack | Insist on portability in joint implementations |
| Partner misrepresents product | Training + annual re-certification |
| Dormant partnerships | Quarterly review and de-tiering |

## 16. International partnerships

- EU: boutique data firms in France, Germany, Netherlands.
- UK: industry analysts and SIs.
- APAC: phase 2, starting Singapore and Australia.
- LATAM: through SIs of global banks and telcos.

## 17. Ecosystem initiatives

- Public ontology library with partner contributions.
- Annual user / partner conference (digital first).
- Joint white papers and webinars.
- Partner awards.

## 18. Anti-patterns

- Announcing a partnership with zero shipping artefact.
- Partners selling us as something we are not.
- Logo-hunting partnerships that never produce pipeline.
- Exclusivity that prevents customer choice.

## 19. Review cadence

- Monthly partner ops meeting.
- Quarterly partner business reviews with top-tier partners.
- Annual partner summit.

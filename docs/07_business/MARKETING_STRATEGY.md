# Marketing Strategy

How we build awareness, educate the market, generate qualified pipeline, and position Ontologia as the category-defining collaborative ontology platform.

---

## 1. Marketing goals by year

**Year 0 (pre-GA / beta)**
- Build a waitlist of 5,000 qualified signups.
- Establish foundational SEO presence (100+ evergreen articles by GA).
- Recruit 10 design partners.

**Year 1 (post-GA)**
- 50,000 website visitors / month.
- 20% signup-to-activation conversion.
- 30% of pipeline originated from marketing.
- Launch events: Product Hunt, Hacker News, YC Launch (if applicable), flagship industry conferences.

**Year 2**
- 200k visitors / month.
- Self-sustaining community of practitioners.
- Recognised as a category-defining brand in knowledge-graph / ontology spaces.

## 2. Brand positioning

Tagline: **"The collaborative ontology platform."**

Supporting narrative:
- We believe knowledge deserves the same care and tooling as code.
- We treat ontologies like living systems — branched, reviewed, versioned, operated.
- We refuse to pretend that AI alone solves the problem. Structure matters.

Tone of voice:
- Clear, technical, confident but humble.
- Never condescending; we respect the reader.
- Evidence over claims; show before tell.
- Honest about trade-offs.

See also: [BRAND_GUIDELINES.md](../04_design/BRAND_GUIDELINES.md).

## 3. Audience segments

- **Practitioners**: ontologists, knowledge engineers, taxonomy / MDM folks, data modellers. Love depth, hate fluff.
- **AI / data leaders**: CDOs, heads of AI / ML / data engineering. Want ROI and control.
- **Builders**: AI engineers, RAG developers, founders. Want speed and API quality.
- **Champions**: anyone senior enough to push a procurement cycle.

Each audience gets dedicated content channels, not separate brands.

## 4. Positioning pillars

1. **Figma for ontologies** — the experience argument.
2. **Git for ontologies** — the engineering argument.
3. **Source of truth for AI** — the business argument.

Every campaign ties back to at least one of these pillars.

## 5. Content strategy

### Content hub: `ontologia.com/learn`
- **Primers**: "What is an ontology?", "RDF vs property graphs", "Why taxonomy drift kills RAG".
- **How-tos**: "Design your first SKU ontology in 30 minutes", "Import from Excel to an ontology".
- **Deep dives**: "Three-way merges for ontologies", "Measuring ontology health".
- **Case studies**: customer stories with concrete before/after outcomes.
- **Comparison pages**: factual, sourced, evenhanded.

### Formats
- Long-form articles (1,500–3,500 words).
- Short posts (500–800 words) for weekly cadence.
- Video walkthroughs and conference recordings.
- Sample ontologies repo on GitHub.
- Interactive demos on the site.

### Content calendar
- 2 long-form articles per week at steady state.
- 1 video per week (engineering or design behind the scenes).
- 1 customer / use-case piece per month.
- Seasonal campaigns tied to conferences and product launches.

### Editorial standards
- Fact-checked; linked sources.
- Every comparison mentions at least one genuine strength of the competitor.
- Claims about performance or cost are reproducible (public notebooks when possible).
- Author by-line and updated-date always visible.

## 6. SEO strategy

- Target high-intent, low-competition keywords first (long tail).
- Cluster pages by topic (ontology authoring, RAG + KG, graph DB ecosystem).
- Technical SEO baseline: Core Web Vitals, structured data, internal linking.
- Build backlinks through guest posts, research reports, open-source contributions.
- Quarterly audit; no black-hat tactics.

Target metrics:
- 50 ranking keywords in top 10 by month 9.
- Organic traffic doubling QoQ for 4 consecutive quarters post-GA.

## 7. Community & developer relations

- Public Slack or Discord for users and practitioners.
- Monthly community call with product updates and open discussion.
- Open-source ontology of the month featurette.
- Hackathons and bounty programmes for examples, plugins, imports/exports.
- Sponsor and speak at: Knowledge Graph Conference, The Graph Forum, Data Council, CIDOC-CRM events, FOSDEM data-&-ML tracks.
- Written-code advocacy: exemplary open-source repos using Ontologia for non-trivial workloads.

DevRel hire planned for month 6–9.

## 8. Product marketing

- Clear positioning docs for every major feature at launch.
- Launch rituals: blog post, video, docs update, email, social, deck refresh.
- Quarterly "State of Ontology" report — a flagship data piece.
- Pricing page polished; clear comparison table between plans; transparent about limits.
- In-app education: tooltips, contextual guides, sample ontologies.

## 9. Campaign playbook

Each campaign has:
- One clear audience and promise.
- Primary content asset (report, tool, guide).
- Supporting content calendar (social, email, partner co-marketing).
- Measurable goals (MQLs, SQLs, signups, attend).
- Post-mortem 30 days after end.

Examples of year-one campaigns:
- **"Ontology fitness check"** — interactive tool evaluating an imported ontology's coverage, cycles, duplication. Lead capture.
- **"Knowledge graphs make RAG smarter"** — evidence-based paper + webinar.
- **"Open ontologies we love"** — curation + interviews + contribution drive.
- **"Migrate from Protégé"** — technical tutorial + migration tool.

## 10. Email marketing

- Double opt-in.
- Frequency cap: 1 newsletter / week + launches + transactional.
- Newsletter mix: engineering, product, customer, community, hiring.
- Segments: practitioners, leaders, builders, customers, lapsed users.
- Metrics: open rate > 35%, CTR > 5%, unsubscribe < 0.5%.

## 11. Paid acquisition — small and focused

- Initial spend: negligible until PLG funnel is healthy.
- Tests: LinkedIn ABM to named accounts, retargeting on key landing pages, Google brand defence.
- Evaluation windows: 30 days with clear kill criteria.
- CAC discipline: target < $3k for self-serve, < $15k for mid-market.

No generic display ads; no programmatic buying; no influencer promos.

## 12. Events & conferences

- **Speak** at 4–6 conferences per year.
- **Sponsor** 2–3 practitioner-heavy events.
- **Host** an annual user conference (digital first, in-person later).
- Side events (dinners, workshops) at major industry gatherings.

Budget and ROI tracked. Every sponsorship has a goal beyond "logo placement".

## 13. Social & community media

- LinkedIn: primary for leaders and founders' voice.
- X / Bluesky: primary for engineers and researchers.
- YouTube: tutorials, conference talks, product walkthroughs.
- Mastodon + federated presence: where practitioner communities live.

We do not buy followers, run engagement pods, or automate "thought leadership" posts.

## 14. Analyst & press

- Analyst list: Gartner, Forrester, IDC for the mid/enterprise play; RedMonk for the dev voice.
- Brief analysts quarterly once traction warrants it.
- Press: launch moments covered by a small group of technical journalists; avoid hype cycles.

## 15. Website & brand experience

- Fast, clean, accessible — practice what we preach (see [ACCESSIBILITY.md](../04_design/ACCESSIBILITY.md)).
- Pages: home, product, use cases, pricing, trust, docs, learn, customers, careers, company, blog.
- Always has a live, interactive demo near the fold.
- SOC 2 and data residency visible on the home page.

## 16. Measurement

### North star metrics for marketing
- Qualified signups / month.
- Activation rate (first commit within 14 days).
- Pipeline sourced by marketing.
- Brand search volume.

### Attribution
- Self-reported "how did you hear about us?" on signup.
- UTMs on all campaigns.
- First-touch and last-touch comparison monthly.
- HubSpot + PostHog for funnels.

### Reporting cadence
- Weekly scorecard.
- Monthly review.
- Quarterly deep dive.

## 17. Anti-hype pledge

- No misleading AI claims ("AI understands your ontology!").
- No fake benchmarks.
- No paid reviews on listings.
- No dark patterns in signup, cancellation, or email consent.
- Customer quotes are verified and updated when relationships change.

## 18. Team & budget

- Year 0: founders + part-time DevRel contractor.
- Year 1: Head of Marketing (generalist with B2B SaaS experience), content writer, DevRel.
- Year 2: PMM, demand-gen specialist, event manager.

Budget scaling follows ARR: 20–30% of planned new ARR in year 1, declining to 15% at scale.

## 19. Alignment with sales & CS

- Weekly joint stand-up (marketing + sales) reviewing pipeline.
- Shared MQL/SQL/SAO definitions.
- Marketing rides on at least 2 customer calls per week.
- CS feedback loop: recurring customer language, friction points, expansion signals.

## 20. Anti-patterns & what we avoid

- "Spray and pray" advertising campaigns.
- Calendars filled with webinars we don't believe in.
- Content that reads like it was written by a robot (and, since mid-2024, often is).
- Targets that reward volume over quality of pipeline.

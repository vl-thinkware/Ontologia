# Beta Program

**Primary owner**: Valentin · **Contributor**: Alexandre · **Status**: Draft v2 (bootstrap-aligned)


How we ran the closed and open betas that led to GA — what we offered, what we asked for, and how we turned beta customers into our first happy, paying base.

---

## 1. Goals

- **Validate product–market fit** with real teams on real problems.
- **Harden the product** under real-world load and edge cases.
- **Build the first reference base** — case studies, quotes, advocates.
- **Inform pricing and packaging** from observed usage.
- **Build a community of practitioners** who feel ownership in Semlify.

## 2. Program phases

| Phase | Duration | Access | Audience size |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Alpha** (internal + trusted advisors) | 6 weeks | By invitation | 20 |
| **Closed beta (design partners)** | 10 weeks | By application | 50 orgs |
| **Open beta** | 8 weeks | Self-serve | Unlimited (caps) |
| **GA transition** | 2 weeks | Everyone | — |

## 3. Alpha phase

### Selection
- Trusted friends of the company, advisors, early hires' networks.
- Non-NDA for direction, light-touch feedback.

### Rewards
- 3 years of Pro at launch pricing.
- Credit in the "thank you" page.

### Deliverables
- Rapid feedback on flows.
- Bugs found and filed.
- Private Slack channel (#alpha) with direct founder access.

## 4. Closed beta (design partners)

The most important phase of the year.

### Selection criteria
- Clear ICP fit (AI / data team; ontology work is painful today).
- Champion with decision influence.
- Willing to meet weekly for 10 weeks.
- Willing to have name and logo referenced if satisfied.
- Diversity of size, industry, geography.

### What we offer
- White-glove onboarding (dedicated session).
- Weekly 45-minute check-ins with product + eng.
- Private Slack channel per partner.
- 1 year of Pro free at end of beta, plus 3-year grandfathered pricing.
- Co-authored case study (optional; opt-out allowed).
- Name on the "launch partners" page (optional).

### What we ask
- Honest, recurring feedback (weekly call + async).
- Bug reports in our issue tracker.
- Data residency choice agreed up front.
- DPA signed early.
- Consent for usage analytics (with workspace-level controls).

### Structure of the 10 weeks
- **Week 1** — kickoff, goals, success criteria.
- **Week 2** — onboarding complete, first commits.
- **Weeks 3–4** — first branch + review workflow adopted.
- **Weeks 5–6** — integrations live (API, export).
- **Weeks 7–8** — governance (roles, branch policies).
- **Weeks 9–10** — close-out, case-study drafting, pricing conversation.

### Cadence
- Weekly call (45 min).
- Weekly async digest from us.
- Monthly executive sponsor call.
- Ad-hoc support via private Slack.

## 5. Open beta

Opens after the closed beta proves core flows.

### Access
- Sign up with email; enter an "Open Beta Access" code sent to the waitlist.
- Cap on signups per week to preserve quality.

### Features
- All GA-scope features flagged on.
- Free tier intentionally generous to drive adoption.
- Billing disabled; clear communication that it will turn on at GA.

### Expectations set
- Bugs are expected; we communicate incidents transparently.
- Data is real but not yet covered by the GA SLA.
- Pricing preview shared 30 days before GA to avoid surprise.

## 6. Governance

- **Beta council**: 5 rotating customers + product + eng + CS. Monthly call, public minutes.
- **Beta roadmap**: visible to all beta participants; they can comment.
- **Feedback triage**: weekly, with product + eng + design.
- **Beta scorecard**: shared in `#betas` internal channel.

## 7. Metrics we track

- Activation rate (first commit within 7 days).
- Time-to-first-branch.
- Time-to-first-review-merge.
- NPS (measured at week 4 and week 8).
- Feature adoption heatmap.
- Crashes and errors.
- Support volume per 100 users.
- Verbatim themes (topic modelling on feedback).

## 8. Feedback tooling

- Canny public board for feature requests.
- In-app "send feedback" widget.
- Per-partner Slack channel.
- Monthly survey (5 questions, 2 minutes).
- Office-hour calls.

## 9. Pricing & transition

### Pricing visibility
- Beta is free.
- Final pricing shared 30 days before GA.
- Grandfathering: design partners get 3 years locked pricing + 1 year free; open-beta paying signups at GA get 20% off first year.

### Conversion
- Auto-convert to Starter or Pro based on actual usage at GA.
- 60-day grace period to pick a plan or downgrade.
- No surprise charges.

## 10. Legal & privacy during beta

- Beta terms addendum signed alongside MSA.
- DPA available from day one.
- Clear marking of "beta" where behaviour may still change.
- No production-critical workflows promised.

## 11. Communication cadence

- Monthly email to all beta participants: product updates, known issues, upcoming changes.
- Ad-hoc breaking changes flagged with 30-day notice.
- Friday digest in community Slack.

## 12. Beta case-study programme

Turn design partners into public references:
1. Quote + logo in the launch materials.
2. Short written case study (1,500 words) on our site.
3. Joint webinar or conference talk (optional).
4. Ongoing reference-call availability (2 calls per year).

In exchange: additional years of Pro pricing locked, swag, priority feature access.

## 13. Anti-patterns we avoided

- Letting beta become a permanent holding pattern.
- Inviting everyone and their dog so feedback becomes noise.
- Shipping half-baked features because "it's beta".
- Treating beta customers as lesser than GA customers.
- Promising features that weren't planned.

## 14. Risks & mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Data loss during instability | Daily backups, frequent restore tests, explicit warning in beta terms |
| Scope creep from loud customers | Roadmap governance; not all feedback becomes a commitment |
| NDAs delaying marketing | Optional attribution; no NDA default |
| Churn at GA pricing | Grandfathered pricing + transparent comms |
| Support swamped | Rotating on-call + deflection via docs |

## 15. Post-beta retrospective

At end of beta, the team produces:
- Beta summary report (users, retention, activation, NPS, feedback themes).
- Decisions locked in (pricing, packaging, roadmap priorities).
- Thank-you gifts to design partners.
- Lessons captured for future beta rounds.

## 16. Ownership

- **PM lead** — owns the overall programme.
- **CS** — owns the per-partner relationship.
- **Eng lead** — owns stability.
- **Marketing** — owns comms and case studies.
- **Founders** — participate directly in every design partner.

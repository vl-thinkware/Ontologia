# Code of Conduct

**Primary owner**: Valentin · **Contributor**: Alexandre · **Status**: Draft v2 (bootstrap-aligned)


Semlify is committed to providing a harassment-free, welcoming, and productive environment for everyone — team members, customers, contributors, and community participants — regardless of gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, ethnicity, nationality, age, religion, or level of experience.

This code applies to every Semlify space: our workplaces, online channels (Slack, Discord, GitHub, LinkedIn), events we host or sponsor, customer interactions, and representations of the company in public.

---

## 1. Expected behaviour

- Be respectful.
- Be kind. Assume good intent.
- Acknowledge others' contributions.
- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Be open to differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the company.
- Give constructive feedback with care.
- Admit mistakes and apologise to those affected.

## 2. Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination in any form.
- Sexualised language or imagery; unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information ("doxxing") without explicit permission.
- Persistent disruption of discussions, events, or work.
- Advocating for, or encouraging, any of the above behaviour.

## 3. Scope

This code applies to:
- All Semlify employees, contractors, advisors, and board members.
- Customers when interacting with our team or community.
- Open-source contributors on our public repos.
- Event attendees at Semlify-hosted or -sponsored events.
- Anyone representing Semlify in public (conference talks, social media).

## 4. Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, report it promptly:

- Internal channel: `conduct@semlify.com`.
- Escalation: CEO directly (valentin@semlify.com — placeholder) if the first channel is inappropriate.
- Anonymous reports: `semlify.com/conduct-report` (managed by a third-party whistleblower service).

Reports are handled confidentially by a dedicated team (People ops + a designated leadership sponsor). We will:

- Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours.
- Investigate fairly, with safeguards against retaliation.
- Communicate outcomes to the reporter and (as appropriate) to affected parties.

## 5. Investigation process

1. **Triage**: confirm receipt, assess urgency.
2. **Fact-finding**: interview reporter, subject, witnesses as needed.
3. **Review**: decision by a panel (HR lead + one senior leader not involved).
4. **Action**: documented decision, communicated to affected parties.
5. **Appeal**: one-time appeal to the CEO or designated independent reviewer.

## 6. Consequences

Violations may result in any of the following, depending on severity and context:

- Private warning and clarification.
- Public apology when appropriate.
- Mediation or facilitated conversation.
- Removal from specific channels, events, or projects.
- Suspension.
- Termination of employment, partnership, or community membership.
- Ban from future participation.

Legal action in cases involving criminal conduct.

## 7. Retaliation

Retaliation against a person who reports in good faith, or who participates in an investigation, is itself a serious violation of this code and of Semlify policy.

## 8. Good-faith reporting

We commit to treating all reports seriously. We also acknowledge that a malicious false report is a harm. Reports found to be made in bad faith with intent to harm may themselves be subject to investigation.

## 9. Leadership accountability

Leaders at Semlify are held to a higher standard of conduct. The same rules apply, and more is expected: modelling the behaviour we want to see, paying attention, and taking action.

## 10. Training & awareness

- New joiners complete a short code-of-conduct training during onboarding.
- Annual refresher training for all employees.
- Conduct-specific training for managers.
- Posters and reminders in shared spaces.

## 11. Working with customers

Customer-facing interactions reflect these values. If a customer behaves in a way that violates this code:

- The team member disengages politely.
- Escalate to CS leadership or CEO.
- We reserve the right to decline service to customers who repeatedly violate this code. Refund or alternative accommodation is offered where appropriate.

## 12. Working with vendors

Our vendors are expected to respect this code of conduct when interacting with our team and our community. Breaches may lead to contract termination.

## 13. Community spaces

Moderators in our public Slack / Discord / GitHub apply this code uniformly. Moderator actions are reviewed by People ops monthly.

## 14. Events

- Hosted events have a dedicated contact for conduct issues.
- Safety team on-call at in-person events.
- Clear signage pointing to reporting channels.
- Zero-tolerance policy enforced immediately when needed.

## 15. Attribution

This code of conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant (v2.1) and the IETF Code of Conduct, with additions specific to Semlify.

## 16. Review

- Owner: CEO + People ops.
- Review cadence: annually, or on material incident.

# Onboarding

The first 30, 60, 90 days at Ontologia. How to land smoothly, do your best work, and feel at home in a remote-first, async-heavy team.

---

## 1. Principles

- **Human first**. Tools and process come after relationships and context.
- **Async by default**. Most decisions happen in docs; meetings are intentional.
- **Ship something small in week 1**. Confidence comes from doing, not watching.
- **Safety to ask**. No one should be stuck for more than a day because they didn't want to ping someone.

## 2. Before day 1 — we prepare

At least 7 days before the start date the hiring manager + People ops:

- Send the welcome email with calendar invites and reading list.
- Ship laptop + peripherals (Mac by default, Linux on request).
- Create accounts: Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub, Linear, Notion, 1Password, Figma, HubSpot, Grafana, Sentry, PostHog, Stripe (role-gated).
- Pair them with a **buddy** (not the manager) for low-stakes questions.
- Announce in `#welcome` with a bio and a fun fact if they shared one.

Security: laptops arrive pre-enrolled with MDM, FileVault on, SSH keys generated on first login, password manager pre-installed.

## 3. Day 1

- Welcome breakfast (virtual or in person depending on location).
- 1:1 with the manager: goals, expectations, cadence.
- 1:1 with CEO (for the first 20 hires, later replaced by a founder AMA).
- Access audit: tickle every tool; note what's missing.
- Read: this page, [CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md), [VISION.md](../00_overview/VISION.md).

## 4. Week 1 — orient

- Finish access setup.
- Shadow a demo and a support call.
- Read the top 10 docs from the `docs/` tree relevant to the role.
- Ship a small PR or write a small doc update.
- Meet 1:1 with one person from each team (eng, product, design, CS, sales).

### Role-specific first-week deliverables
- **Engineer**: submit a low-risk PR (e.g. polish a log line, improve a test).
- **Designer**: propose one micro-improvement to the design system.
- **PM**: write a 1-pager on an area you now own.
- **CS**: respond to a user message with manager shadowing.
- **Sales**: complete product certification, listen to 5 call recordings.
- **Marketing**: publish a short blog post (co-edited).

## 5. Weeks 2–4 — integrate

- Own a full slice end-to-end.
- Attend all hands, roadmap reviews, and team stand-ups.
- Write a "first impressions" doc (template in `docs/09_team/templates/first_impressions.md`) with what surprised you, what works, what feels off.
- Pair with a tenured teammate on a harder task.
- Deliver one customer-visible change (feature flag, bug fix, doc, demo).

## 6. Days 30–60 — contribute

- Take ownership of a workstream.
- Propose or write an RFC.
- Run a meeting you normally attend.
- Start an in-depth review on a neighbouring area to broaden context.
- Collect feedback from 3 teammates using the feedback template.

## 7. Days 60–90 — operate

- Deliver a measurable outcome (shipped feature, closed deal, launched campaign, published doc set).
- Complete certification exams if role requires (sales product, support, security training).
- 360° feedback round.
- Define personal OKRs for the next quarter with manager.

## 8. Tooling crash course

| Tool | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Slack | Real-time chat, but kept light; channels over DMs. |
| Linear | Source of truth for engineering and product work. |
| Notion | Docs, meeting notes, OKRs, handbooks, RFC archive. |
| GitHub | Code + reviews. |
| Figma | Design system + prototypes. |
| Grafana / Sentry | Observability. |
| HubSpot | CRM. |
| PostHog | Product analytics. |
| 1Password | Secret storage; never re-use passwords. |
| Plain / Intercom | Customer support. |

Never copy production data into sandboxes or personal devices.

## 9. Meetings & rhythms

- Company all hands: weekly, 30 min.
- Team stand-up: daily, 10 min (async preferred).
- Product review: weekly, 45 min.
- Roadmap review: bi-weekly, 60 min.
- Retrospective: monthly.
- OKR review: quarterly.

Calendar etiquette: block focus time, "no-meeting Wednesdays", prefer async documents to "quick chats".

## 10. Async communication norms

- Use Slack for short, ephemeral messages.
- Use Notion / GitHub for anything that might matter in a month.
- Do not expect replies after 6 pm local time.
- Tag the smallest necessary audience.
- Prefer "reply in thread".
- Decisions with lasting impact require a short written doc.

## 11. Compensation & benefits

- Transparent bands; published internally.
- Market-rate salary + meaningful equity.
- Health insurance where laws allow (US, UK, EU covered; other geos via EOR).
- Unlimited PTO with **minimum** 25 days expected.
- $2,500 yearly learning budget.
- $500 home-office stipend per year.
- Twice-yearly team gatherings.
- Paid parental leave (16 weeks minimum).

See the internal People handbook for specifics (localised per country).

## 12. Working hours & flexibility

- Remote-first; core hours 10 am–3 pm local.
- 2 overlapping hours with the rest of the team required.
- No surveillance software; we trust adults.
- Results over hours.

## 13. Reviews & growth

- Monthly 1:1 with manager (60 min).
- Quarterly "How are you doing?" check-in (30 min).
- Twice-yearly formal performance review.
- Personal growth plan signed off quarterly.
- Career ladders documented for engineering, product, design, CS, sales, marketing.

## 14. Feedback culture

- Radical candour with kindness.
- Positive feedback in public; constructive feedback in private.
- Feedback is a gift and is expected both ways (to managers included).
- 360° reviews twice a year.

## 15. Psychological safety

- Say "I don't know".
- Write down the question.
- Escalate without fear.
- Mistakes owned, not hidden.
- No blame for honest errors; process failures investigated, people supported.

## 16. Decision making

- RACI or DACI for meaningful decisions.
- RFC process for anything lasting (technical or strategic).
- Disagree and commit: once a call is made, row in the same direction.
- Bring data; bring stories; bring silence sometimes.

## 17. Diversity, equity & inclusion

- Active sourcing for under-represented groups.
- Inclusive hiring panels.
- Quarterly DEI review with leadership.
- Pay equity audit twice a year.
- ERGs encouraged as team grows.

## 18. Security onboarding

- Complete security training within 30 days.
- Pass the internal phishing simulation.
- Enrol MFA on every tool.
- Acknowledge acceptable-use policy.
- Read [SECURITY.md](../06_security_compliance/SECURITY.md).

## 19. Leaving well

We hope you stay. If you don't:

- No bad-leaver shame.
- Exit interview with People ops and manager.
- Equity per vesting schedule; no acceleration traps.
- Access revoked same day; laptop returned.
- Reference letter offered for good-leavers.

## 20. Ownership

- **People ops lead** owns onboarding program.
- **Hiring manager** owns the individual experience.
- **Buddy** owns day-to-day friction removal.
- Feedback loop: post-onboarding survey at day 30 and day 90, reviewed quarterly to improve the program.
